Caramel Apples

Home


Description:

Incredibly delicious caramel apples! The caramel coating is very gooey, so refrigerate the apples for about 15 minutes, or until the caramel has firmed up.

Ingredients:

  • 6 firm apples, such as Granny Smiths
  • 6 wooden craft sticks
  • cooking spray
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 (14 ounce) package individually wrapped caramels, unwrapped
  • toppings of your choice such as chopped nuts, colourful sprinkles, crushed oreos, melted milk or white chocolate, candy bar pieces, toasted coconut flakes etc.

Steps:

  1. Gather the ingredients. Remove the stem from each apple and press a craft stick into the top. Spray a baking sheet with cooking spray.
  2. Place caramels and milk in a microwave-safe bowl; microwave for 2 minutes, stirring once. Set aside to cool briefly.
  3. Roll each apple quickly in caramel sauce and your chosen toppings until well coated. Place on prepared sheet to set.
  4. Enjoy~

Recipe tips:

  • We recommend a tart or sweet-tart apple variety for this recipe, such as Granny Smith apples, because the tart flavor will perfectly balance the sweet caramel. Other good options include Honeycrisp, Gala, or Fuji apples. Avoid softer apple varieties like Red Delicious or McIntosh.
  • Remove any wax on the apples to help the caramel stick better: Soak apples in hot (but not boiling) water with a little lemon juice, scrub gently, then dry well.
  • Store the cooled and set caramel apples in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two weeks. If you have lots of leftovers, consider packaging and gifting them. Simply wrap each apple in cellophane and tie with a festive ribbon.
